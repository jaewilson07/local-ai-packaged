Getting started creating your first YouTube video, or even just a tutorial for internal consumption can feel overwhelming, but it doesn't have to be!¬† Even the name of the activity, "Content Creation" seems like the side hustle everyone has but no one likes! :D

This article is your cliff notes of all the things to consider when you get started in content creation.¬† But my most important tip?¬† **Just press record.**

*Record the video.¬† Post it (you can use a BS title).¬† Close your laptop and tackle the rest tomorrow. *

In the morning, for a more formulated approach, I've documented some of the learnings I picked up over the way.

## 3 reasons why you should get started in Content Creation

-
**You can point to your expertise and community engagement when it's time to go job-hunting, or grovel for a raise.**¬† Anyone can say "I am an expert."¬† But it's quite another thing to be able to point at 15 blog posts, 100+ YouTube Videos and a visible history of community engagement.¬† *Even if your content isn't something you can post publicly, post them as private videos and private articles so you have one polished place you can send your boss or a recruiter to.*

-
**Creating content is a great way of solidifying learning.**¬† Every time I learn something new, I¬† try to make a video about it or at the very least a publishable set of notes.¬† If you have to explain something you learn it.

-
**Supporting community helps grow the community and builds goodwill next time you need help on a project.¬† **Who knows, that goodwill might

For me personally, *when I went job hunting, in several cases I skipped the technical screening because I had demonstrated expertise.*¬† Usually, it boiled down to whether people liked my presentation style and thought I'd be a good fit for the role.¬† Honestly, I found myself driving a lot of interviews because I knew exactly what kind of work I wanted to do and I needed to know the new organization would be a good fit for me.
